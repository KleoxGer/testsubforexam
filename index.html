CYBER CRIME

What is cyber crime?

Cybercrime

Cybercrime refers to criminal activities that are carried out using computers, networks, and the internet. It involves the unauthorized access, theft, manipulation, or destruction of data and information.

Types of Cybercrime

  • Hacking: Unauthorized access to computer systems or networks to steal or manipulate data.
  • Phishing: Sending fraudulent emails or messages to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.
  • Identity Theft: Stealing someone's personal information, such as social security number or bank details, to commit fraudulent activities.
  • Malware: Distributing malicious software like viruses, worms, or ransomware to infect and damage computers or networks.
  • Online Scams: Deceptive schemes that aim to defraud individuals or organizations, such as fake online auctions or lottery scams.
  • Cyberstalking: Harassment or stalking behavior carried out online, often through social media platforms or email.
  • Child Exploitation: The use of digital platforms to produce, distribute, or access explicit content involving minors.

Impact of Cybercrime

Cybercrime can have severe consequences:

  • Data breaches and financial losses for individuals and organizations.
  • Damage to reputation and loss of trust in online services.
  • Disruption of critical infrastructure, such as power grids or communication networks.
  • Privacy violations and loss of personal information.
  • Emotional distress and psychological harm to victims.

It is important to stay vigilant, adopt strong security measures, and educate oneself about online threats to prevent falling victim to cybercrime.
